Life In Brief

After running an historic campaign against the late Senator John McCain, Barack Obama was inaugurated as the 44th president of the United States, on January 20, 2009, as the first African-American to serve the highest office of te land.

KEY EVENTS

February 17, 2009

Digging Us Out of the Recession - American Recover and Reinvestment Act of 2009

With the recession in full gear, Barack Obama and his adminstration helped champion a bill to revive the American economy and create millions of new jobs.



March 23, 2010

The Affordable Care Act - Healthcare for all Americans

A landmark bill in attempting to reduce healthcare inequality in the United States, the Affordable care act lowered costs healthcare costs, provided a public option, and assisted the socio-economically disadvantaged and minority groups with access to healthcare.



Taking Out the Trash Bin - Operation Neptune Spear

Osama Bin Laden, the man responsible for perpetrating America's greatest tragedy, had been in hiding for years, escaping the hands of U.S. forces. Under Obama's leadership, the adminstration and military carried out a daring raid to infiltrate Bin Laden's Pakistani compound and bring justice to the thousands who had lost their lives to his crimes.
