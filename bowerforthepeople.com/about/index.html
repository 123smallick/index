Meet Bower

Bower is an advocate, a policy-maker, an educator, and a fighter. On November 6, 2016, Bower was elected as a Junior Senator in the U.S. Senate, making her the first woman of Canadian origin to be elected Senator in the history of the United States. Bower believes that the goal of our nation is to educate and support the new generation of Americans and that a diversity of voices in the political process is essential to crafting more effective public policy.

Born in an unkown, foreign country named Canada, Bower knew from the beginning that globaly education needed reform. As an educator, at numerous educational institutions, the values of promoting civic engagement and of encouraging future generations to think critically are deeply instilled in her ideology. Thanks in large part to her the influence of students in her career, Ayanna has always been acutely aware of the role that government can play in lifting up families and communities.

Ayanna moved to the United States before the 1990's to attend Plano Senior High School. AS a result of several years as an educator, Bower’s professional life mirrored her commitment to service and lifting the voices of those in our community that are represented the least: students.

In 2016, she launched a historic at-large campaign for the Texas Senate seat and won. As a leading voice in the student activism movement, Senator Bower worked in partnership with residents, advocates, and other elected officials to combat the inequities and disparities facing students and recent graduates across the country.

A leader of the Opposition against backwards, DeVos era policies, Bower has been dedicated to the kind of bold, activist leadership she emphasized throughout her groundbreaking campaign. She has introduced legislation to target predatory student loan companies, to support students as they graduate and enter the workforce, and to fundamentally reimagine our institutions by implementing policies to support trade and vocational schools, among much else. And she has been a vocal advocate on behalf of our immigrant communities, led the creation of the comprehensive Criminal Justice reform, fought to create safe environments in our inner cities, and to hold the White House accountable.

In 2019, Bower was named Times “Person of the Year” and, in early 2020, she was one of Forbes' top 100 under 100. She has been honored by the Greater Dallas Chamber of Commerce as one of their Ten Outstanding Leaders. In 2015, she was named a Fullbright Fellow and distinguished Rhodes Scholar. She is also an Aspen-Rodel Fellow in Public Leadership.

Bower lives in the DFW area with her husband, dog, and cat.
